# Security Policy #3: Security Incident Response

## Purpose of Policy

The purpose of this policy is to develop the response to and reporting of security incidents, including:

- The identification of and response to suspected or known security incidents.
- The mitigation of the harmful effects of known security incidents, to the extent possible.
- The documentation of security incidents and their outcomes.

> **Note:** Breach definitions, remediation steps, and breach notification requirements vary between federal regulations (HIPAA, GLBA, etc.) and between individual states. It is highly recommended to consult with breach experts or legal counsel to determine The Company's responsibilities.

---

## Definitions

**Breach** — The acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of personally identifiable information (PII) or sensitive company data (such as email, employee information, confidential information, etc.) which compromises the security or privacy of the PII or sensitive company data.

**Unsecured PII** — PII that is not rendered unusable, unreadable, or indecipherable to unauthorized individuals through the use of a technology or methodology such as encryption. The definition of unsecured PII varies between different federal and state regulations.

---

## Reporting and Response

- The Company will ensure that all incidents, threats, or violations that affect or may affect the privacy, confidentiality, integrity, or availability of PII and sensitive company data will be **reported and responded to**.
- The Company shall have a **Security Incident Response Team (SIRT)** charged with identifying, evaluating, and responding to security incidents. The Privacy Security Officer shall oversee SIRT activities.
- The SIRT will investigate all known or suspected privacy and security incidents.
- The SIRT will document a procedure for all employees to follow to report incidents (see Appendix A – Security Incident Response Log).
- All employees must receive training on how to identify and report security incidents.
- All employees must follow the documented procedure to report incidents and must report all known or suspected security incidents.
- All employees must assist the SIRT with security incident investigations.

---

## Breach Determination

The SIRT will investigate all reported and suspected security breaches and refer to applicable federal and state regulations to help with breach determination. Breach determination varies significantly between regulations and between states.

---

## Breach Notification

If the SIRT determines that a breach of unsecured PII has occurred, breach notification of affected individuals may be required.

### Key Elements of a Breach Notification

**Date of Discovery** — Usually the first day the breach is known, or would have been known with reasonable diligence.

**Timeliness of Notification** — The Company will provide required notifications without unreasonable delay. The timeframe varies by regulation.

**Content of Notification** — If required, notifications must include:
- A brief description of what happened, including the date of breach and date of discovery.
- A description of the types of unsecured PII involved.
- Steps individuals should take to protect themselves from potential harm.
- A description of what The Company is doing to investigate the breach, mitigate harm, and prevent further breaches.
- Contact procedures for individuals to ask questions or learn additional information (telephone number, email address, website, or postal address).

The notification should be written in **plain language**.

---

## Methods of Notification

**Written Notice** — First-class mail to the individual's last known address, or via email if the individual has agreed to email notice. If the individual is deceased, notification is usually sent to next of kin or a personal representative.

**Substitute Notice** — If contact information is out of date, a substitute notification may be used, typically a conspicuous posting on The Company's website home page or conspicuous notice in major print or broadcast media in areas where affected individuals likely reside. The notice should include a contact phone number.

**Notification to Media** — In addition to notifying individuals, notification to the media may be required.

**Notification to Federal or State Regulatory Agencies** — The Company may need to report breaches to applicable regulatory agencies.

**Notification by Third Party Service Providers** — Third Party Service Providers responsible for a breach of The Company's PII should be required to notify The Company within a pre-determined timeframe as defined in the Service Provider Agreement.

---

## Appendix A — Security Incident Response Log

All security incidents should be documented in the Security Incident Response Log or the Security Incidents Module in the Security Portal.
