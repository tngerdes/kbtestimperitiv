# Security Policy #5: Network Security

## Purpose of Policy

The purpose of this policy is to describe the physical safeguards applicable for each server, desktop computer system, and wireless computer system used to access, transmit, receive, and store PII and sensitive company data to ensure that appropriate security is maintained and that access is restricted to authorized employees.

---

## Network Security Requirements

The Company will take reasonable and appropriate steps to prevent unauthorized access to workstations, servers, and portable devices (including laptops, smartphones, CD-ROMs, DVDs, USB drives, etc.) that store or access PII and sensitive company data.

- Workstations and laptops in common areas should be physically placed so that unauthorized people cannot view confidential information such as logins, passwords, PII, and sensitive company data.
- Workstations and laptops in common areas should utilize **privacy screens** to prevent unauthorized viewing.
- Workstations and laptops in common areas should be secured with **locking cables** or similar restraints.
- To the extent technically feasible, all portable devices containing PII and/or sensitive company data should be **encrypted**. Encryption should also be used when sending PII across public networks, wireless networks, email, and the Internet.
- Portable devices and media should be **concealed from view** when offsite to prevent theft.
- All network servers, application servers, routers, database systems, device management hardware, and other servers should be located in a **physically secured room or area**, accessible only to authorized personnel via lock and key or other appropriate security mechanism.
- All workstations, servers, and portable devices must run **anti-virus/anti-malware software** with current and up-to-date virus/malware definitions. Employees must not disable these tools unless specifically directed by IT support.
- A **network firewall** should be in place and up to date. Firewalls should be monitored and alerts should trigger in the event of unauthorized or suspected intrusion.
- **Log files** from network equipment (firewalls, servers, desktops, laptops, and other devices) should be stored and retained. Retention length may vary depending on federal, state, or industry regulations.
- All workstations, servers, and portable devices must implement a **security patch and update procedure** to ensure all relevant patches are promptly applied based on vulnerability severity.
- **Periodic network vulnerability scans** should be performed on all internal and external (internet-facing) systems. Known vulnerabilities should be remediated. After remediation, an external penetration test should be performed to confirm unauthorized external access is prevented.
- Workstations must not be located where they will be directly affected by extremes of temperature or electromagnetic interference. Precautions should also be taken to protect against utility-related threats (water, sewer, steam lines, etc.).
- All facilities storing systems with PII and/or sensitive company data should have appropriate **smoke and/or fire detection devices**, sprinklers or approved fire suppression systems, and accessible fire extinguishers.
- All servers containing PII and/or sensitive company data should be connected to an **Uninterrupted Power Supply (UPS)** to prevent crashes during power outages. Servers should be configured to shut down in a controlled manner during extended outages.
- All systems should be connected to **surge protectors** where feasible.
- A **user identification and password authentication mechanism** shall be implemented to control user access. See *Security Policy #6 – Access Control*.
- Employees who suspect any inappropriate or unauthorized use of workstations should **immediately report** the incident to the Security Officer.
