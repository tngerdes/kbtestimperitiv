# Security Policy #10: Facility Security Plan

## Purpose of Policy

The purpose of this policy is to define the procedures that will limit physical access to PII and sensitive company data and the facility or facilities in which such systems are housed, while still ensuring that proper authorized access is allowed.

---

## Facility Security Requirements

- **Physical security** of office buildings must be implemented to protect PII and sensitive data as well as other company assets. Physical measures may include: alarm systems, surveillance cameras, fences, locked gates/doors, etc.

- All systems that store or access PII and/or sensitive company data should be stored in **locked rooms, closets, or cabinets** to prevent unauthorized access. Access to these facilities should be minimized and limited only to employees and/or vendors who need access to perform their job function.

- Where practical, all **visitors** should be restricted from areas where files or systems containing PII and/or sensitive company data are stored. Alternatively, visitors must be **escorted or accompanied** by an approved employee in any area where such files or systems are stored.

- A **clean desk policy** will be implemented and includes the following:
  - All employees are prohibited from keeping unsecured paper files containing PII and sensitive company data in their work area when they are not present (e.g., during lunch breaks).
  - At the end of the day, all files containing PII and/or sensitive company data must be stored in a **locked filing cabinet, desk drawer, or other locked location**.
  - Any systems that store or access PII and/or sensitive company data should be **closed or logged off** when unattended.

- The **Security Officer** shall maintain a secured and confidential **master list** of all lock combinations, passcodes, and keys. This list will identify which employees possess keys, keycards, or other access devices and confirm that only approved employees have been provided access credentials.

- Where practical, all visitors expected to access areas other than common space or areas containing PII and/or sensitive company data should be required to **sign in with a Photo ID** at a designated reception area, where they will be assigned a visitor's ID or guest badge, unless escorted at all times.
