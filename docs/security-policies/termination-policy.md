# Security Policy #2: Termination Policy

## Purpose of Policy

This policy defines the steps required to revoke both physical and system access to The Company's facilities and network resources.

**Termination of Access:** It is essential that supervisors and/or Information Technology (IT) terminate access to company facilities and systems in a timely manner to protect the information, systems, and resources. Supervisors/IT are required to terminate access **immediately upon termination** (or even before, when possible) of the employee, workforce member, or contractor.

---

## Access Revocation Requirements

Upon termination, the following steps must be taken:

1. **Physical access revocation** — A terminated employee shall be required to surrender all keys, IDs, access cards/codes or badges, business cards, parking permits, and the like, that permit access to The Company's premises or information.

2. **Electronic access revocation** — A terminated employee's physical and electronic access to PII and sensitive company data must be **immediately blocked**.

3. **Return of records** — Terminated employees must return all records containing PII and sensitive company data, in any form, that may be in the former employee's possession (including information stored on laptops, portable devices, media, files, records, work papers, etc.).

4. **Revoke all computer, network, and data access**, including:

   **Internal systems:**
   - Microsoft Windows / Network Domain
   - Systems that store or access PII and sensitive company data
   - Email
   - Database applications
   - Any other systems the terminated employee has access to

   **External systems:**
   - Cloud-based systems such as credit card processing, billing, CRM, etc.
   - Remote access must be removed
   - Wireless access must be removed

5. **Documentation** — All termination steps that are taken should be **documented and retained** for legal purposes and/or federal or state regulations.
