# How to Share SharePoint Files from File Manager

This guide explains how to share files and folders stored in SharePoint directly from Windows File Explorer.

## Prerequisites

- Your SharePoint files must already be synced and visible in File Explorer via OneDrive.
- You must be signed in with your company account.

## Steps

1. **Open File Explorer** on your taskbar.

2. In File Explorer, **find the file or folder** you want to share. Then, right-click on it. A drop-down menu will appear. Click **Share**.

3. The sharing pop-up will appear. Click on the **"People you specify can view"** button (wording may vary based on your setup).

4. **Choose your sharing settings** (your company name will appear in place of "Imperitiv"):

   - **Anyone with the link** — Allows anyone who has the link to access it.
   - **People in "[Your Company Name]" with the link** — Allows anyone within your company who has signed in with their company account to access it.
   - **People with existing access** — Allows anyone who already has access to the whole site to access it. Use this option when you want to direct someone to a file or folder without granting new access.
   - **Specific people** — Only people you choose can access it. Type their full email address for external users; for internal users, type enough of their name for it to auto-populate.

5. You can also check or uncheck the **"Allow editing"** option to provide read-only or edit access. Note: if you allow editing, you cannot block the download at this level.

6. When finished, click **Apply**.

7. If you wish to send a message, click in the **"Message…"** area and type your message, then click **Send**.

   > **Important:** The email sent via this method may appear as spam to the recipient. It is recommended that you instead open your own email account (Outlook), create a new email, and paste the link manually.

8. To copy the link, click the **Copy** button. A new window will appear — click the new **Copy** button and paste the link into an email you compose manually in Outlook. Once sent, you have completed the process.
