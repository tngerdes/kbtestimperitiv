    Security Policy #1: Written Information Security Policy (WISP)

    Statement of Policy

    The objective of The Company in the development and implementation of this comprehensive Written Information Security Policy (WISP) is to create effective administrative, technical, and physical safeguards for the protection of personally identifiable information (PII) of customers, clients, and employees, as well as sensitive company information that could be harmful if unauthorized access were to occur.

    The WISP sets forth a procedure for evaluating and addressing electronic and physical methods of accessing, collecting, storing, using, transmitting, and protecting PII and sensitive company information.

    The use of the term employees includes all of The Company's owners, managers, employees, all independent contractors, and temporary employees.

    Purpose of Policy

    The purpose of the WISP is to:

    1. Ensure the security and confidentiality of PII of customers, clients, employees, or vendors, as well as sensitive company data (e.g., emails, confidential company information, employee information).
    2. Protect against any reasonably anticipated threats or hazards to the security or integrity of such information.
    3. Protect against unauthorized access to or use of such information in a manner that creates a substantial risk of identity theft, fraud, or harm to The Company.

    Scope of Policy

    In formulating and implementing the WISP, The Company has:

    • Identified reasonably foreseeable internal and external risks to the security, confidentiality, and/or integrity of any electronic, paper, or other records containing PII and sensitive company data.
    • Assessed the likelihood and potential damage of these threats.
    • Evaluated the sufficiency of existing policies, procedures, and safeguards.
    • Designed and implemented a WISP that puts safeguards in place to minimize identified risks.
    • Implemented regular monitoring of the effectiveness of those safeguards.

    Security Safeguards

    Administrative Safeguards

    Security Officer — The Company has designated a Security Officer responsible for:

    • Implementation of the WISP and all provisions outlined in Security Safeguards.
    • Training of all employees who may have access to PII and sensitive company data (annual training for existing employees; training at hire for new employees).
    • Regular monitoring of the WISP's safeguards and employee compliance.
    • Evaluating Third Party Service Providers' ability to implement appropriate security measures, and requiring contractual compliance.
    • Reviewing all security measures at least annually, or whenever there is a material change in business practices.
    • Investigating, reviewing, and responding to all security incidents or suspected incidents.

    Security Management — All security measures will be reviewed at least annually, including performing a security risk assessment, documenting results, and implementing recommendations.

    Minimal Data Collection — The Company will only collect PII that is necessary to accomplish legitimate business transactions or comply with regulations.

    Information Access — Access to records containing PII and/or sensitive company data shall be limited to persons whose job functions require legitimate access.

    Employee Termination — Terminated employees must return all records containing PII and sensitive company data. Physical and electronic access must be immediately blocked. Keys, IDs, access codes/badges, and business cards must be surrendered. See Security Policy #2 – Termination Policy.

    Security Training — All employees will receive annual security training. New employees should be trained as part of the onboarding process. Employees must pass an exam demonstrating their knowledge. Documentation of training should be retained.

    WISP Distribution — A copy of the WISP is to be distributed to each current and new employee. Employees must acknowledge receipt in writing or electronically. See Appendix A – WISP Employee Acknowledgement Form.

    Contingency Planning — All systems storing PII and/or sensitive company data should have data backed up nightly, encrypted, and stored offsite. Disaster Recovery mechanisms and documented procedures should be in place. A system criticality assessment should define how critical each system is. Backups and recovery procedures should be tested periodically.

    Security Incident Procedures — Employees are required to report suspicious or unauthorized use of PII and/or sensitive company data. Post-incident reviews must be conducted. See Security Policy #3 – Security Incident Response.

    Emergency Operations — Procedures should define how The Company will respond to emergencies, including employee contact information, critical vendor contacts, account information, and emergency operating procedures.

    Data Sensitivity Classification — All data should be categorized by its sensitivity level. PII and sensitive company data should be highly protected; publicly accessible information requires minimal protection.

    Third Party Service Providers — Any service provider permitted access to PII and/or sensitive company data must be required to protect it. Service agreements must contractually hold them responsible.

    Sanctions — Disciplinary actions will be taken for violations. See Security Policy #4 – Sanction Policy.

    BYOD Policy — If employees are allowed to use personally owned devices, proper safeguards must be implemented. See Security Policy #9 – BYOD Policy.

    Physical Safeguards

    Facility Access Controls — Physical security must be implemented to prevent unauthorized access. All systems accessing or storing PII must be physically locked. Employees must maintain a "clean desk" and properly secure PII when away from their desk. Visitors will be restricted from areas containing PII. See Security Policy #10 – Facility Security Plan.

    Network Security — Safeguards include: isolating systems that access PII, encryption on all portable devices, anti-malware on all systems, network firewalls, periodic vulnerability scans, capturing and retaining network log files, and ensuring servers are stored in an environmentally safe location. See Security Policy #5 – Network Security.

    Technical Safeguards

    Access Control — Access to PII and sensitive company data shall be restricted to approved active users and accounts only. Unique user accounts and passwords must be assigned. Automatic logoff procedures must be implemented. See Security Policy #6 – Access Control.

    Computer Use — All employees will be given a Computer Use Policy defining acceptable and unacceptable use of computing resources. See Security Policy #7 – Computer Use.

    Data Disposal — Written and electronic records containing PII shall be securely destroyed or deleted at the earliest opportunity consistent with business needs or legal retention requirements. See Security Policy #8 – Equipment Disposal.

    System Activity Review — All systems storing or accessing PII should log and store system activity. Periodic reviews should identify unauthorized access. See Security Policy #3 – Security Incident Response.

    Encryption — To the extent technically feasible, all portable devices containing PII should be encrypted. Encryption should also be used when sending PII across public networks, email, and wireless networks.

    Appendix A — WISP Employee Acknowledgement Form

I am aware that violations of the WISP may subject me to disciplinary action and may include termination of my employment.
