    Security Policy #9: Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Policy

    Purpose of Policy

    The purpose of this policy is to develop appropriate safeguards to protect PII and sensitive company data on employee personally owned devices. Proper security controls are essential to protect sensitive information on these devices. Documented instructions and requirements must be provided to all employees who may access or store PII and sensitive company data on personally owned devices, and acknowledgement of acceptance must be documented and retained.

    Policy

    • The Company may be responsible for breaches of PII and may suffer consequences of breached sensitive company data resulting from unsecured employee personally owned devices.
    • Employees must be aware that breaches or inappropriate use of their devices that put PII and/or sensitive company data at risk may negatively affect customers, The Company, and the employee themselves. The Company has the right to revoke access to PII and/or sensitive company data or levy sanctions per the sanction policy.
    • Encryption usually offers a safe harbor under federal and state regulations and is the strongest protection against a data breach. Encryption should be used on all devices that access or store PII and/or sensitive company data.
    • Employees are not permitted to access PII and/or sensitive company data on personally owned devices unless authorized and approved. Only approved and properly configured devices will be granted access.
    • The Company will limit who has access to PII on personally owned devices, providing only the minimum access necessary to perform job functions.
    • The organization and their IT group/provider will work together to manage and enforce this policy.

    Procedure

    • Employees must request permission to use personally owned devices and complete the registration form.
    • The Company and IT will periodically review and update this policy when new requirements are implemented or security requirements change. Employees must be notified of changes and provide signed acknowledgement.
    • The Company and IT reserve the right to monitor and inspect registered devices to ensure PII and sensitive company data are being properly protected.
    • Upon an employee's termination, The Company and IT will ensure that any devices with PII and/or sensitive company data are returned to IT for final analysis and removal of PII/sensitive data or applications that access such data. This will be conducted as soon as possible.
    • Documentation, acknowledgement, and registration forms will be retained in the employee's personnel folder.

    Security Requirements

    • All devices must be password protected.
    • Passwords must be complex — minimum 6 characters, combination of upper- and lower-case letters, numbers, and symbols.
    • Devices must lock after five (5) incorrect password attempts.
    • Devices must "time out" and require a password after 5 minutes of inactivity.
    • Text messages containing PII and/or sensitive company data must be sent through the secure texting application provided. If no secure texting app is provided, employees should not send PII via text.
    • Emails containing PII and/or sensitive company data must be sent encrypted. If secure email encryption is not provided, employees should not send PII via email.

    Restrictions and Limitations

    • "Rooted" or "Jailbroken" devices are not permitted to access PII and/or sensitive company data.
    • Employees must notify management when selling, trading in, recycling, or disposing of their personal devices.
    • The employee's device may have data remotely deleted/wiped if:
    • The device is lost or stolen.
    • The employee terminates their employment.
    • IT detects a data or policy breach, virus, or similar threat.
    • Lost or stolen devices must be reported to management and/or IT within 24 hours.
    • Employees must inform management and/or IT if they plan to upgrade, recycle, or dispose of their device.
    • Employees who voluntarily resign must present their device(s) to IT within 48 hours to have all PII and sensitive company data removed.
    • Employees who do not turn over their device within 48 hours after voluntary resignation are subject to a full remote wipe of all data on their device.
    • For scheduled terminations: Terminated employees must present their device(s) to IT on the day of termination. Employees who fail to do so, after being given the opportunity, are subject to a full remote wipe.

    Sanctions

    Violations or abuse of this policy are subject to the repercussions laid out in The Company's sanction policy.
