    Security Policy #7: Computer Use

    Purpose of Policy

    The purpose of this policy is to ensure that employees understand what functions should and should not be performed on The Company's computers and network to maximize the security of PII and sensitive company data. The policy also provides guidance regarding proper safeguards when accessing social media sites.

    Computer Use Policy

    • The Company may provide workstations and other computer systems to employees for the purpose of performing their job functions. Employees shall be responsible for using workstations appropriately in conformance with this Policy.
    • The Company may remove or deactivate any employee's user privileges — including user access accounts and access to secured areas — when necessary to preserve the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of its facilities, user services, and data.
    • Employees must be assigned and use a unique User ID and Password. See Security Policy #6 – Access Control.
    • Employees using The Company's information systems and workstation assets should have no expectation of privacy. The Company may log, review, or monitor any data stored or transmitted on its information system assets.

    Appendix A — Computer Use Policy

    Introduction

    Computers, the Internet, and electronic mail (email) are powerful research, communication, and time-saving tools available to employees. Like any tools, they have the potential to be used for inappropriate purposes.

    Policy

    The following policies on computer, Internet, and email usage shall be observed by all employees:

    • Users must comply with all appropriate laws, regulations, and generally accepted Internet etiquette.
    • The primary purpose of the Internet and email is to conduct official business.
    • Users should identify themselves properly, conduct themselves professionally, and be aware that their activities reflect on the reputation and integrity of the company.
    • Each user is individually responsible for the content of any communication sent over or placed on the Internet and email.
    • All employees have a responsibility to ensure a respectful workplace. Computer equipment must not be used to visit Internet sites that contain pornographic or sexually explicit information, pictures, or cartoons.
    • Exceptions are only allowed when pre-approved by supervisors or company management for official business, research, or investigatory work.

    Prohibited Actions

    The following actions are prohibited. It is unacceptable for employees to:

    1. Knowingly or intentionally publish, display, transmit, retrieve, or store inappropriate or offensive material on any company computer system.
    2. Create or distribute defamatory, false, inaccurate, abusive, threatening, racially offensive, or otherwise biased, discriminatory, or illegal material.
    3. View or distribute obscene, pornographic, profane, or sexually oriented material.
    4. Violate laws, rules, and regulations prohibiting sexual harassment.
    5. Engage in any unauthorized activities for personal financial gain.
    6. Place advertisements for commercial enterprises.
    7. Download, disseminate, store, or print materials including articles and software in violation of copyright laws.
    8. Download any software (including games, screen savers, toolbars, or browsing tools) without the permission of supervisors, company management, or IT staff.
    9. Violate or infringe on the rights of others.
    10. Conduct business unauthorized by the company.
    11. Restrict or inhibit other users from using the system or reduce computer system efficiency.
    12. Cause congestion or disruption of networks or systems, including distribution of chain letters.
    13. Transmit incendiary statements or promote the use of weapons.
    14. Use the system for any illegal purpose or contrary to company policy or business interests.
    15. Connect a personal computer to the company network without having it checked by IT staff.
    16. Monitor or intercept the files or electronic communications of other employees or third parties.
    17. Hack or obtain access to systems or accounts they are not authorized to use.
    18. Disclose a Login ID or password to anyone, or allow anyone to access any information system with someone else's credentials.
    19. Use other people's Login IDs or passwords to access any information system for any reason.
    20. Post any PII or sensitive company data on social network sites or public forums, including pictures of PII or customers without permission.
    21. Remove electronic media containing PII or confidential/proprietary information unless authorized by a supervisor or company management.

    Acknowledgement

    Any employee who abuses the privilege of access to email or the Internet in violation of this policy will be subject to corrective action, including possible termination of employment, legal action, and criminal liability.

    By signing the Computer Use Policy, I agree to comply with its terms and conditions. Failure to read this Agreement is not an excuse for violating it. The Company may deny access to information systems if this Agreement is not returned signed and dated.
